Late Shrikant Verma

Shrikant Verma was born in 1931 in Bilaspur, Madhya Pradesh. He graduated from Nagpur University with a Master of Arts degree in Hindi.

Verma began his career as a journalist and worked for several leading newspapers and magazines. In 1976, he entered politics and was elected to the Indian Parliament as a member of the Indian National Congress party.

Verma served as a Member of Parliament for two terms and was also a member of the Union Cabinet.

Verma was a prolific writer and published over twenty books of poetry and prose. His poems are known for their simplicity, beauty, and social commentary.

Verma was also a strong advocate for social justice and equality. He died in 1986 at the age of 54 in New York due to cancer.
